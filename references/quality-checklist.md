# Quality Checklist

Run this checklist before presenting a completed case study or set of CV bullets to the user. Flag any items that don't pass.

---

## Case Study Quality Checklist

### Structure
- [ ] All essential sections present (1, 2, 4, 5, 8, 9, 10, 12)
- [ ] Sections flow logically — a reader unfamiliar with the project can follow
- [ ] No section is just a list of items without explanation
- [ ] Context section makes the company and project clear in under 30 seconds

### Specificity
- [ ] Every section shows reasoning, not just reporting ("we chose X because Y")
- [ ] Collaborators are named with their roles (not just "the team")
- [ ] Method choices are explained (why this method, why not alternatives)
- [ ] Numbers are present where possible (sessions, participants, stakeholders, %, timelines)
- [ ] No jargon left unexplained — someone outside UXR can follow

### Synthesis Section (the one that matters most)
- [ ] Describes what raw data looked like at the start
- [ ] Shows the steps from data → codes → themes → insights
- [ ] Includes at least one moment where initial framing broke or thinking shifted
- [ ] Names who was involved in synthesis
- [ ] Does NOT just say "we identified themes" or "patterns emerged"

### Impact Section
- [ ] Ties outcomes to concrete decisions, behaviour changes, or metrics
- [ ] Addresses at least two of: product impact, decision-maker impact, ways-of-working impact
- [ ] If impact was preventing a wrong move, that's named explicitly
- [ ] Does NOT just say "stakeholders liked it" or "the research was well received"

### Voice & Tone
- [ ] Consistent with the user's chosen voice throughout
- [ ] Reads like a person wrote it, not a template filled in
- [ ] Personal introduction (section 1) includes something human, not just job title
- [ ] Reflections (section 12) are honest, not just "everything went great"

### Completeness
- [ ] No placeholder text remaining
- [ ] Artifact/image placeholders marked with 📎 where visuals should go
- [ ] All claims could be defended in an interview ("tell me more about that")

---

## CV Bullet Quality Checklist

- [ ] Every bullet follows the formula: power verb + what + quantifier + consequence
- [ ] No fluffy or obvious language ("end-to-end research process" alone = weak)
- [ ] Every claim is quantified where possible
- [ ] No bullets start with "Helped" or "Assisted" or "Participated in"
- [ ] Each bullet makes one clear point (not everything crammed together)
- [ ] Consequences are concrete (not "improved the product" — how? by how much?)
- [ ] No bullets could apply to any researcher at any company — each is specific to this person's work

---

## Common Issues to Flag

**"This could be anyone's case study"**
If you replace the company name and it still reads the same, the content is too generic. Push for specific details, names, numbers, and decisions.

**"Lots of what, no why"**
Sections that list activities without explaining reasoning. For every "we did X," there should be a "because Y" or "which led to Z."

**"The synthesis black box"**
Data goes in, insights come out, nothing in between. This is the #1 weakness in case studies. Push for the messy middle.

**"Impact without attribution"**
Claiming business metrics improved without showing the connection to the research. It's OK to say "contributed to" — but explain the chain.

**"Reflections that aren't reflections"**
"Everything went well" or pure positives. Real reflections name what was hard, what you'd change, what surprised you.
